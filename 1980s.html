The 80s

1981 Saucony

Saucony worked with podiatrist Frank Santopietro to create a lightweight, breathable shoe that would fit in with the most on-trend form of running. They called it the Jazz, and it became the building block for future Saucony models. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Nike Air Force 1

1982 marked the beginning for the legendary Nike Air Force 1. Earning the nickname "Uptowns" on basketball courts across New York City, and that they "started the sneaker culture as we know it today," referencing continuous releases of new colorways decades later. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Vans Checkerboard Slip-on

Appeared on the big screen in "Fast Times at Ridgemont High." The style was "memorialized" in the movie that year, and she said it was inspired by the designs kids were drawing on their sneakers. In 2019 they can be seen on the street and red carpets alike. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT & StockX

1984 Adidas Micropacer

It integrated computer technology with athletics and featured a microsensor in the toe that tracked distance, running pace, and even calorie consumption. Wearers could see collected information on the screen built into the left tongue. [1]

Courtesy of Bata Shoe Museum

Air Jordan 1

1985's most iconic sneaker spot. The pair appeared on the court in 1984 and were actually banned from the NBA, but Michael Jordan continued to wear them anyway. MJ wore the shoes during the 1984-1985 NBA season. [1]

Courtesy of Sneaker History & Sole Collector

1986 Converse Weapon

The design offered maximum stability, and it was worn by both Larry Bird and Magic Johnson, two basketball greats, during the '86 NBA Finals. Magic Johnson wore the Weapon in the LA Lakers colorway seen here while playing for the team. [1]

Courtesy of Converse

1987 Nike Air Max 1

The 1987 Nike Air Max 1 was designed by Tinker Hatfield and inspired by the Pompidou Center in Paris. He said Hatfield loved the "inside-out" design of the building, which inspired the window on the heel of the shoe, making the cushioning tech visible. 1988 Nike Air Jordan 3

The 1988 Nike Air Jordan 3 "remains one of the most celebrated" pairs of Air Jordans to date.They were the first Jordans to feature the Jumpman logo, and the printed leather met MJ's desire for a shoe that looked instantly worn-in. The retro of this style featured the Nike Air logo on the heel. [2]

Courtesy of Sneaker History


1989 Reebok's Pump

In 1989 Reebok's Pump sneaker debuted at a then-obscene price tag of $170, which helped to put it on the map. The sneaker's pumping mechanism was used to make the most custom-fit shoe possible, was inspired by the tech in ski boots.The circle on the tongue is what wearers press to pump up the inner padding.
