The 70s

Adidas Stan Smith

The 1971 Adidas Stan Smith was a rebranding of the 1964 version of the shoe designed for Robert Haillet. Later versions of the shoe featured minor detail changes. [1,4]

Courtesy of Sneaker History

Nike Cortez

This Cortez style was the first of the iconic brand and was designed by Bowerman himself. Before the Cortez, Knight and Bowerman's brand was called Blue Ribbon Sports. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Puma Clyde

They were created for New York Knicks player Walt "Clyde" Frazier, who wanted to put his own spin on the already iconic Puma Suede. Which made them considered the first signature basketball sneaker in history. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Nike Waffle

Bowerman was trying to create a shoe with the best traction of any on the market and started experimenting with his wife's waffle iron. It resulted in the waffle pattern on the bottom of the shoe above. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT


Vans Era

In 1976, Vans collaborated with skateboarders to create The Vans #95, which has become known as the Era style. The padded ankle lining and variety of colorways have helped make it the "shoe of choice for a generation of skateboarders." [1]

Courtesy of GOAT & Vans

1977 Bata x Wilson x John Wooden’s

Today, it remains one of the most sought-after sneakers by sneaker collectors. The signature shoes were available for only one year and featured a revolutionary polyurethane sole, making it lightweight, reducing fatigue, and increasing endurance. [1]

Courtesy of Bata Shoe Museum

1978 Onitsuka Tiger Tai Chi

Originally became famous after Bruce Lee wore them with a matching tracksuit in the movie "Game of Death." They resurfaced and relaunched onto the market after Uma Thurman wore a similar outfit in the 2003 film "Kill Bill." [1]

Courtesy of Asic & GOAT
