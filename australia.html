LeBron 8

In 2010 basketball shoes hit a peak. LeBron James had made the move to Miami and Nike was celebrating with a "South Beach" colorway of the LeBron 8 shoe. The bright colors and scarce availability made this drop "the perfect storm of excitement." [1]

Courtesy of Sneaker History

Air Jordan x Vashtie Kola

In 2010 Vashtie Kola, a New York City street style influencer, became the first female to collaborate on an Air Jordan retro. Kola completely "made it her own." making it one of the most iconic sneakers from the first year of the new decade [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Concepts x New Balance 999 Hyannis

A 2011 icon, the shoe paid homage to Hyannis Port in Massachusetts, as both Concepts and New Balance are New England-based brands. He said this was the first of many collaborations that would come from the duo. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Air Yeezy 2

In 2012 West released his second sneaker with Nike: the Air Yeezy 2. This shoe was even more popular than the rapper's first Nike release, mainly thanks to his rising social presence, influence, and relationship with Kim Kardashian. [1]

Courtesy of Sneaker History

Atmos x Nike Air Max 1

They released that year was "one of the most memorable Maxes to date," It combined a woodland camouflage print with snakeskin texture to create the "Animal Camo" style for the sneaker. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Adidas x Rick Owens Runner

The 2014 Adidas x Rick Owens Runner was the perfect combination of both brands: an impeccably crafted, functional running shoe and a sleek, structural, artistic design. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

FENTY x Puma

2015 FENTY x Puma Creeper one of the most iconic sneakers of that year. Footwear News reported that the shoe — which was the first collaboration between the singer and athletic brand — was so hyped up that it sold out within three hours of dropping. [4]

Courtesy of GOAT

2015 Adidas x Un-Backed

Collaboration with a UN-backed non-profit: Parley for the Oceans. It was the first of a larger collaboration project and featured an upper made entirely from recycled plastic and illegal gillnets — or fishing equipment — found in the ocean. [1]

Courtesy of Adidas

2015 Common Projects

This 2015 sneaker launched its brand, Common Projects, into the realm of "cult status," The minimalist design, low-batch yet high-quality manufacture, and "cryptic" numbering system stamped in gold on the side heel make The Achilles the epitome of "stripped-down luxury." [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Adidas Ultraboost

Another 2015 icon is the Adidas Ultraboost. It was one of the brand's "most successful sneakers of the modern era," using tech that was "rumored to be developed by NASA and BASF." It was dubbed the "world's greatest running shoe" by the brand itself. [1]

Courtesy of Sneaker History

2016 Pharrell Williams x NMD Human Race

The 2016 Pharrell Williams x Adidas Originals NMD Human Race brand was iconic on many levels. Not only were they comfortable and functional — they combined the Boost tech with a sock-like upper — but they encouraged positivity through colorways and in-your-face wording. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Yeezy 350v2 "Yebra"

Farfetch wrote that the 2017 Zebra Yeezy Boost 350 V2 might be "the most quintessential Yeezy sneaker ever." A product of Kanye's move from Nike to Adidas, this sneaker is an evolved play on the Ultraboost. Farfetch reports Stadium Goods — a sneaker resale hub — calls this shoe and colorway "one of the best Yeezy Boost 350s to exist." [1]

Courtesy of Sneaker History

KAWS X Air Jordan

In 2017 Jordan Brand called on famed street artist Brian Donnelly, commonly known as KAWS, to design a variation of the Air Jordan 4. The KAWS signature art and "XX" on the heels turned these classics into works of art themselves. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT

Travis Scott x Air Jordan

In 2018 Travis Scott collaborated on an Air Jordan 4 sneaker under the name of his record label, Cactus Jack. A Houston native, he took color inspiration from the Houston Oilers football team — now the Tennessee Titans — and added some personal flair. [1]

Courtesy of GOAT
