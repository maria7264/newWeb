Adidas Gazelle

The Adidas Gazelle was introduced as a soccer shoe. Later on, they became what would refer to today as a lifestyle sneaker — they were being worn "in the pursuit of fashion rather than just fitness,"[1]

Photo - Courtesy of Adidas

Adidas Superstar/Pro Model

The influence on fashion and the birth of the Superstar, Run DMC’s first non-athlete sneaker deal and more. It went from solving one little problem to changing sneaker culture forever in the blink of an eye, and it still remains a staple selection to this day.[4]

Puma Suedes

First introduced as a basketball sneaker. Becoming legendary after Olympian Tommy Smith became the first man to run 200 meters in less than 20 seconds. Soon after, the Suede became "a staple in the hip-hop world and a go-to for b-boys breakdancing"[1]

Photo - Courtesy of Puma

Adidas Superstar Low

The Adidas Superstar made waves as the first low-cut, leather basketball sneaker. It also earned the nickname "Shell Toe" because of the shape of the shoe's toe area. [1]

Photo - Adidas



Onitsuka Tiger Corsair

The shoe that’s influenced all of modern running came from the Onitsuka Corsair. It was this sneaker that Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight modified with an EVA wedge at the rear, rebranded and sold under their own name, the Nike Cortez, that really changed running. As influential as the Cortez was, without the Tiger, there probably would be no Nike, at least, not as we know it today. [4]
